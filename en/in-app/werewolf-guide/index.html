Welcome

This guide was put together by players who have spent countless hours playing Werewolf and want to share what they've learned. Think of it as advice from a friend who's been there — someone who can point out the little things that make a big difference.

Plato covers the rules, but there's so much more to discover: how to read other players, when to speak up, how to deceive convincingly, and what experienced players expect from each other during a match.

Playing Nice Goes a Long Way

After thousands of matches, players have naturally developed some ground rules that keep games fun and fair for everyone. Nobody likes a match where roles are revealed instantly or where one player ruins the experience for others.

Simple things like waiting until the last second to vote (so you don't give away too much information early), or knowing when to speak up versus when to listen, can completely change how much you and others enjoy the game. These aren't official rules — just good habits that make every match better.

Seer

Good Role

Ability

Every night, the Seer can check a player to know which team they belong to. Roles like Alpha Wolf, Shapeshifter, Jester, and Lycan partially or totally affect the authenticity of the information this role can provide.

Strategy and Tips

  • Read everything said carefully to know who to check.
  • Do not reveal information unnecessarily. Make sure there are no roles like Wolf Shaman before revealing info.
  • Have an attitude and make yourself heard.

Advanced Play

The Seer can make risky plays like fake claiming/accusing falsely to pressure a player. If they reveal themselves instantly upon being accused, it is key to back down and give the real information.

Pro Tips

Level up your game

Last-Second Voting

Vote at the very end of Day 1. This prevents revealing multiple roles and keeps the game balanced and fun for both teams. It also helps develop your unconscious role-reading skills—when you vote, you need some idea who you're voting for, training your brain to associate roles with behaviors.

Pre-Type Everything

Write your night action before the timer runs out. Send it at the last second to prove your role and prevent stealing.

"I am Doctor, saving PlayerX"

Read the History

Check the voting history and chat. Contradictions and patterns reveal wolves. Your individual history—what you said, how you voted, who you defended—is your shield. Being able to sell your coherence is how you survive.

Baiting

Fake a valuable role at night to attract wolf attacks. Useful for Hunter, Knight, or King to protect real important roles. If a bait worked before, stick to that posture—but don't lie so much that your own team punishes you.

"Bard here, charming PlayerX"

As a role like Hunter, send this 5 seconds before night ends. Make sure the role isn't in the game, or be ready to explain yourself afterwards.

General Strategies (Day & Night)

Knowing what each role does isn't enough—you need to know how to behave. Every decision matters. We divide strategies into day and night phases.

Day Strategies

The day is when everyone talks, accuses, defends, and votes. Master this phase by staying calm and appearing believable.

Villagers

  • Never trust blindly. Even convincing players might be acting. Always doubt and evaluate.
  • Analyze contradictions and silence. Wolves might stay too quiet or talk too much to manipulate.
  • Don't force reveals. Asking everyone to reveal roles only helps wolves.
  • Hold suspicions. Speaking too early lets suspects adapt. Timing is everything.
  • Think before voting. Last-second voting on Day 1 isn't just tradition—it's tactics that creates useful chaos.

Wolves

  • Appear as a villager. Build trust without looking forced. Don't stand out too much or disappear.
  • Participate like you're good. Share opinions, suggest theories, express doubts.
  • Defend teammates subtly. But remember: sometimes sacrificing a teammate secures the win.
  • Maintain coherence. Logical mistakes expose you. If you lie, repeat your story.
  • Vote strategically. Sometimes letting a fellow wolf die cleans your image.

Night Strategies

Night isn't a pause—it's the most technical moment. What happens in those seconds can bring decisive advantage or downfall.

Villagers

  • Pre-type with clarity. Include your role, target, and brief justification. Speed matters.
  • Mislead with baits. Use fake role claims to attract wolf attacks and protect real valuable roles.
  • Protect key roles. If you know who has an important role (like Seer), keep them alive at all costs.
  • Repeat successful patterns. If a bait worked before, stick to it. But don't confuse your own team.

Wolves

  • Deceive with fake pre-types. The most dangerous wolves look good during nights.
  • Plan impactful kills. Choose targets that break information chains or cause panic.
  • Change style if suspected. Adjust behavior, build a role identity, become more active.
  • Exploit good team mistakes. Sometimes you don't need to lie—just plant doubt around their errors.

Common Mistakes

Avoid these patterns that hurt your team:

Talking Too Much

Revealing unnecessarily or over-explaining makes you look defensive.

Staying Silent

Not participating out of fear. Better to say something reasonable than nothing.

Impulse Voting

Voting without basis or on personal dislike ruins matches.

Poor Pre-typing

Typing late or with mistakes reduces credibility and risks role stealing.

Play After Death

Dying is not losing. A good player can leave the path prepared for their team even after death.

Your final words and votes can still help your team or bury it. Don't leave the game immediately—understand that you're playing beyond your own survival. Understanding this means playing beyond your ego.
